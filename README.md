## Electric Vehicle Charging Analysis
## Project Overview
This project analyzes electric vehicle (EV) charging data to understand demand patterns, congestion, renewable energy integration, and smart grid stability.
The workflow uses SQL for data preparation and Power BI for interactive dashboards.

## Tools
SQL → Querying, aggregating, and cleaning the dataset.

Power BI → Visualizing insights with charts, KPIs, and dashboards.

## Dataset Highlights
Each charging session includes:

Station info: load, queue length, electricity price.

Vehicle info: type, battery capacity, energy consumed.

Context: traffic density, weather condition, renewable energy ratio.

Time factors: arrival time, charging duration, peak/off‑peak slot.

## Power BI Visuals
KPI Cards → Total energy consumed, avg station load, renewable energy ratio, optimization reward.

Scatter Plot → Waiting time vs queue length (shows congestion).

Grouped Bar Chart → Station load by location (Urban/Highway) and time slot (Peak/Off‑Peak).

Bar Chart → Station load by weather and traffic density.

Treemap → Revenue contribution by station ID.

Pie Chart → Energy consumed by vehicle type (Car, Bus, Two‑Wheeler).

Line Chart → Renewable energy ratio variation by day.

## Key Insights
Urban stations face higher peak demand than highway stations.

Congestion increases waiting times as queues grow.

Renewable energy supply doesn’t always match charging demand.

Dynamic pricing influences charging behavior.

Weather and traffic conditions affect station utilization.

## Why It Matters
By combining EV charging data with renewable energy insights, this project shows how smart grid integration can:

Reduce costs and emissions.

Improve charging efficiency.

Support sustainable transportation systems.
